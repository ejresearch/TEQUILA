## Vocabulary (5 points)

1. **Fill in the blank:** The Latin letter "A" is pronounced as "___".  
   **Correct Answer:** a) ah  
   **Explanation:** In Latin, the letter "A" is pronounced as "ah," similar to the "a" in "father." This pronunciation is consistent and does not change based on word position.

2. **Multiple Choice:** What is the English meaning of the Latin word "Amicus"?  
   **Correct Answer:** b) Friend  
   **Explanation:** "Amicus" translates to "friend" in English. This word is often used to describe a person with whom one shares a bond of mutual affection.

3. **Fill in the blank:** The Latin letter "B" is pronounced as "___".  
   **Correct Answer:** a) beh  
   **Explanation:** The Latin "B" is pronounced as "beh," similar to the English "b" in "bed." It is a voiced bilabial plosive.

4. **Multiple Choice:** Which Latin letter is pronounced as "keh"?  
   **Correct Answer:** b) C  
   **Explanation:** In Latin, the letter "C" is always pronounced as "keh," like the "k" in "kite," regardless of its position in a word.

5. **Fill in the blank:** The Latin word "Bonus" means "___" in English.  
   **Correct Answer:** b) Good  
   **Explanation:** "Bonus" translates to "good" in English. It is often used to describe something of high quality or moral virtue.

## Grammar & Chant (5 points)

6. **Short Response:** Write the first four letters of the Latin alphabet.  
   **Correct Answer:** A, B, C, D  
   **Explanation:** The Latin alphabet begins with A, B, C, and D, similar to the English alphabet. These letters are foundational in learning Latin phonetics.

7. **Multiple Choice:** Which of the following is a hard sound in Latin?  
   **Correct Answer:** a) C as in "Civis"  
   **Explanation:** The letter "C" in Latin is always hard, pronounced like "k" in "kite," as in "Civis" (citizen).

8. **Fill in the blank:** The chant helps us remember the Latin alphabet. What comes after "A" in the chant?  
   **Correct Answer:** B  
   **Explanation:** The chant sequence is A, B, C, D, and so on. This helps students memorize the order and pronunciation of the Latin alphabet. Rhythm tip: Clap or tap to keep a steady beat as you chant.

9. **Short Response:** Explain the difference between the hard and soft sounds of the letter "C".  
   **Correct Answer:** Hard 'C' as in 'Civis', soft 'C' as in 'Cicero'  
   **Explanation:** In Latin, "C" is always hard, like "k." In English, "C" can be soft, like "s" in "Cicero," but this does not apply to Latin pronunciation.

10. **Fill in the blank:** In the chant, the letter "D" is pronounced as "___".  
    **Correct Answer:** deh  
    **Explanation:** The Latin "D" is pronounced as "deh," similar to the "d" in "dog." It is a voiced dental plosive.

## Translation (4 points)

11. **Translate to English:** "In principio"  
    **Correct Answer:** In the beginning  
    **Explanation:** "In principio" literally translates to "in the beginning." It is a phrase often used to denote the start of something, famously used in the Bible.

12. **Translate to Latin:** "Good"  
    **Correct Answer:** Bonus  
    **Explanation:** The English word "good" translates to "bonus" in Latin. This adjective describes something positive or beneficial.

## Virtue Reflection (1 point)

13. **Short Written Response:** How can practicing discipline help you learn Latin better? Write one or two sentences.  
    **Sample Response:** Practicing discipline helps you study regularly and improve your skills. By setting aside time each day to review Latin, you build a strong foundation and make steady progress.
