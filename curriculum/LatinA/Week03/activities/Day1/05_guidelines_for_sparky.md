# Week 1 Day 1: First Declension Nouns: Introduction

## Objectives & Success Criteria
### Objectives
- Identify first declension noun endings.
- Recite the first declension chant.
- Translate simple sentences with first declension nouns.

### Success Criteria
- Students correctly identify noun endings.
- Students recite the chant from memory.
- Students translate simple sentences accurately.

## Materials & Setup
- Chant chart (from WeekSpec)
- Flashcards (vocabulary_spiral + new vocab)
- Parsing worksheet (cases, numbers)

## Minute-by-Minute Flow

### Phase 1: Greeting & Spiral Review (5 min)
**Sparky says:**
"Salvē! Today we'll practice **Patience** as we learn new Latin endings together."

**Activity:**
- Show flashcards: "puella," "schola," "mensa," "liber," "aqua."
- Ask: "What does 'puella' mean?" (Hint: It's a person in the garden.)
- Recycle grammar: "Can you find the noun in 'Puella est in horto'?"

**Check for Understanding:**
- "What is the ending for 'puella' in nominative singular?"

---

### Phase 2: Chant Introduction/Practice (5–7 min)
**Sparky says:**
"Great job! Let's try that again. Let's chant it once slowly, then once with rhythm."

**Activity:**
- Display chant chart: "-a, -ae, -ae, -am, -ā; -ae, -arum, -is, -as, -is."
- Model chant (once slowly, once at tempo).
- Students echo.
- Point to endings as they chant.

**Check for Understanding:**
- "Can you recite the chant?"

---

### Phase 3: Grammar Instruction (8–10 min)
**Focus:** First declension noun endings, simple sentence translation

**Sparky says:**
"Remember, practice makes perfect! Which case tells who/what does the action?"

**Activity:**
- Introduce first declension endings using the chant.
- Show example sentence: "Puella est in horto."
- Parse together: "Puella" (nominative singular).
- Highlight misconception: Incorrect noun ending.
  - Correction cue: "Check the chant for the correct ending."

**Check for Understanding:**
- "What does 'mensa' mean?"

---

### Phase 4: Guided Practice (5–7 min)
**Sparky says:**
"Let's say the chant together. Try the sentence pattern A before B."

**Activity:**
- Present practice sentences: "Puella est in horto," "Mensa est in aula."
- Students parse/translate with scaffolding.
- Differentiation:
  - **Support:** Use visual aids for endings.
  - **Extension:** Create sentences using new nouns.

**Check for Understanding:**
- "Translate: 'Puella est in horto.'"
